FLORA-2: Knowledge Representation & Reasoning with Objects, Actions, and Defaults

FLORA-2 is an advanced object-oriented knowledge base management system and application development environment. It is a dialect of F-logic with numerous extensions, including meta-programming in the style of HiLog, logical updates in the style of Transaction Logic, and defeasible reasoning. FLORA-2 was designed with extensibility and flexibility in mind, and it provides strong support for modular software design through its unique feature of dynamic modules.

Applications of FLORA-2 include intelligent agents, Semantic Web, knowledge-bases networking, ontology management, integration of information, and more.

FLORA-2 comes with comprehensive manuals and a tutorial, which provide an overview of theoretical foundations as well as of the FLORA-2 language and system. The tutorial is accompanied by programming examples and exercises.

FLORA-2 relies on the XSB inference engine, which must be installed in order to run FLORA-2.

News

June 30, 2013:  A new release showed up in the wild: Version 0.99.1 (Lotus). Several enhancements, bug fixes, and, notably, simplified installation. As always, details are in the release notes.

May 13, 2013:  After more than 5 years of development under the sponsorship of Vulcan Inc., FLORA-2 Version 0.99 (Water Lily) has been released! This release includes many enhancements and extensions, including defeasible rules, user-defined functions, delay quantifiers, rule Ids, and more. Details are in the release notes. The license has also changed from LGPL 2.0 to Apache 2.0.

May 11, 2013:  The CVS repository is now obsolete and is no longer maintained. From now on, all development will be using the SVN repository at Sourceforge.
